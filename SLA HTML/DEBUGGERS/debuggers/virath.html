History is the study of past events, particularly those involving human societies, cultures, and civilizations. It helps us understand how the world has evolved over time by examining causes, consequences, and patterns in human behavior. From ancient myths and oral traditions to modern scholarly research, history has transformed into a disciplined field that relies on evidence, critical analysis, and interpretation. Historians use primary sources like documents, artifacts, and eyewitness accounts, along with secondary sources such as books and articles, to reconstruct narratives and explain historical developments.
