🏫 Current challenges faced by 10th & 12th students in Tamil Nadu

1. Overcrowded classrooms & teacher shortages

  • Government schools saw an enrollment surge of around 6 lakh students in Classes 2–12 post-pandemic, straining infrastructure and leaving classes with 60+ students where teacher-student ratios remain poor
  • Official audits estimate a shortage of 11,711 classrooms, and at the current pace, this deficit may persist for a decade or more The Times of India

2. Lack of sufficient facilities

  • Many government schools lack proper laboratories, libraries, drinking water, and separate toilets. Some classes are held under trees or in temporary shelters The New Indian Express.

3. Academic support gap

  • In growing student cohorts, government students lag behind private school peers—e.g. only 35% average in Class 10 English vs. 57% for private school students News18
  • Low conceptual grasp persists: one teacher relayed that even Class 8 students struggled with basic division Reddit

4. Digital divide & inadequate technology

  • Although smart‑classrooms and hi‑tech labs are being rolled out, many schools still lack reliable Internet, well‑trained staff, or even devices per student Republic World

🛠 Tamil Nadu’s planned improvements for 10th & 12th students

1. Infrastructure investment

  • Under the Perasiriyar Anbazhagan School Development Scheme, ₹7,500 crore is earmarked over five years, including ₹745 crore in 2024‑25 to construct 3,032 classrooms, 26 labs, 133 toilets, and 59 drinking-water points in high/higher secondary schools
  • In Budget 2025‑26, a total of ₹55,261 crore has been allocated to education, with ₹1,000 crore specifically for improving government school infrastructure (more classrooms, science labs, drinking water facilities)

2. Smart classrooms & technology labs

  • Plans to build up to 2,676 smart classrooms in government schools at ~₹65 crore, along with upgrades for 2,000 computer labs and 880 hi‑tech labs by 2025‑26

Curriculum

1. Curriculum reform & remedial support

  • Syllabus and textbooks across Classes I–XII are due for a complete overhaul over the next three years, with Kannada/English/Tamil trim from Class 10 textbooks to reduce student stress
  • The Thiran initiative delivers targeted remedial coaching for Classes VI–IX, addressing literacy and numeracy gaps before they become entrenched in higher classes The Times of India

2. Teacher recruitment & academic guidance

  • Over 1,721 post‑graduate and 841 graduate teachers are to be recruited via the Teachers Recruitment Board soon—helping to reduce class sizes and workloads

Career

Career guidance

  • Career guidance begins early via the Naan Mudhalvan scheme from Class IX, helping students prepare effectively for higher education and competitive exam

1. Health & Student welfare

  • The Chief Minister’s Breakfast Scheme provides free nutritious meals to primary-level students to support retention and attention in class; expanding nutrition indirectly supports older students too by improving school culture
